DYNAMO FLASHLIGHT
This is our Key Feature, the Player's best tool for visibility, and its only way to defend itself from what lurks in the dark...
DYNAMO: Battery

The battery goes through a few check before deciding if it should be draining or not.

We start by verifying that it's actually toggled ON. It wouldn't make much sense to drain the battery while it's turned OFF.

If the flashlight is ENABLED, we then check if the player is currently recharging its flashlight. If that is the case, we simply add to the current amount of battery, pulse the luminosity of the flashlight to mimic Dynamos in real life, and rotate the Hand Crank.

If the battery is NOT actively being recharged by the player, we verify that they aren't charging a FLASH. We then freeze the battery if they are.
Then, if all of the flags listed above are set, we DRAIN the battery at a set speed, defined by a Serialized Field on the Flashlight object.

While the BATTERY is being DRAINED, we also update the LUMINOSITY of the FLASHLIGHT, as well as the battery indicator on the Player's CURSOR at the center of the screen.

FLASHLIGHT: Flash Beacon

With enough battery in your Flashlight, holding SPACE lets you charge up a FLASH.

This is your unique defense against the monsters roaming the halls of our game.

Holding the FLASH key charges up a flash, but letting go too early prevents it from firing, making it charge back down.

Charging up a flash reduces the intensity of the flashlight by a LOT, and plays an animation to emphasize the fact that it is currently charging an attack.

Releasing the KEY after charging a FLASH for long enough will let out a MASSIVE BURST OF LIGHT from your flashlight. This burst pushes away smaller enemies, and stuns larger ones. Unfortunately, releasing a FLASH drains the entirety of your Battery, leaving the Player vulnerable and unable to see.

Each enemy has a different reaction to being flashed, defined by their 'OnFlashed' Function, overriden directly from a common parent MonsterController script.
