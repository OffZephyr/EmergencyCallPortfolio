NPCs and NARRATION
This page contains explainations for all of the logic for our NPCs, Dialogue System, Subtitles and other Prompts that may be given to the player.
NPC CHARACTERS: Behavior

NPC BEHAVIORS are all defined by SequenceObjects, and only during or after scripted sequences. OUTSIDE of scripted sequences, NPCs will stick to whatever task was given to them last (Mutually Inclusive: e.g. standing still where they currently are, walking or running to a specific location, looking at a specific location, following the player, etc.).

Without the use of SequenceObjects, NPCs will never stray off course, and will always stick to whatever they are currently doing.

NPCs also inherit the same 'CharacterHandler' parent class as the Player character, used to animate head angles, walk/run cycles and other movements they have in common.

DIALOGUES: Scriptable Object

This here is the Class Diagram showing our DialogueElement Scriptable Object.

This object was made to be as simple as it could possibly be, allowing anyone on the team to write the dialogues for an entire Cutscene or Sequence.

At this core, this object is a big list containing each line of dialogue. Each line is composed of 4 elements:

  • NAME ("character") :
    The character's name, also used to find the NPC talking and make it play an animation.
  • DIALOGUE LINE ("text") :
    The written subtitle for this line of dialogue that appears on the player's screen
  • SUB LINE ("subText") :
    A second layer of text, used when monsters are speaking
  • VOICE ("voice") :
    A sound effect, played when the line appears on the Player's screen

These objects are then called at the start of a sequence containing dialogue. Sequences go down the list in order, using events placed by hand inside of the AnimationClip they run for precise timing.

HUD: Dialogues & Action Prompts

Both Dialogues and Control Prompts are triggered manually, though Dialogues can only be triggered by SequenceObjects, while Control Prompts are triggered at any time if they are needed.

Both work in similar ways, as they are both called from the same place. We just need to call the function and give it the right variables (a DialogueElement in the case of dialogues, and a string for Prompts).

The HUD script will then play the corresponding animation to make the text appear on the player's screen, and make it disappear when the right conditions are met.

Only one DialogueElement and one Prompt can be active at once on the player's screen.

The text will then clear itself on its own once it isn't needed anymore.
